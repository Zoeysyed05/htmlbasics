What is bootcamp

A "bootcamp" is a short, intensive training program designed to quickly equip individuals with practical skills in a specific field, often focusing on in-demand career areas like coding, data analysis, or digital marketing; it

it typically involves a project-based learning approach, demanding schedule, and hands-on experience to prepare participants for immediate application in the workplace, drawing its name from the rigorous nature of military basic training, also called "boot camp.". Key points about bootcamps: Intensive and fast-paced: Bootcamps are designed to deliver a high volume of information and skills in a relatively short time frame. Practical focus: They prioritize hands-on learning through projects and real-world applications, rather than theoretical knowledge.
